Where to Start

For new players, the best place to start depends on what kind of experience they want. Visual novels and interactive story games can feel very different from one another, so it helps to begin with a style that matches your interests.

  • For mystery and strong characters: Phoenix Wright: Ace Attorney Trilogy
  • For psychological tension and darker storytelling: Doki Doki Literature Club Plus! or Raging Loop
  • For science fiction and route-based storytelling: STEINS;GATE or AI: The Somnium Files
  • For quiet atmosphere and emotional exploration: Florence or What Remains of Edith Finch
  • For players who like puzzle and narrative design together: Cube Escape Collection or Ghost Trick: Phantom Detective

These games are very different in tone, but each one shows a different path into interactive storytelling. A good starting point is not always the most famous game, but the one that matches the kind of story you most want to experience.
