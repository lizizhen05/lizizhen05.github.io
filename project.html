My Visual Novel Project

I am especially interested in creating a visual novel inspired by an idea I have been developing called Riverse, or Reverse-Flow River. The story centers on a river that flows backward, symbolizing obsession, memory, and the desire to return to the past.

In this project, the river is not only part of the setting, but also part of the emotional structure of the story. The more tightly a person holds onto regret and longing, the more strongly the river continues to flow. When that attachment begins to fade, the river also begins to disappear.

I want this visual novel to focus on time loops, changing relationships, and the emotional cost of trying to rewrite what has already happened. Rather than telling a simple linear story, I am interested in multiple routes, repeated choices, and endings shaped by memory, love, and release.

What draws me to visual novels is that they can combine writing, atmosphere, music, and symbolism. A project like this would let me explore not only narrative design, but also the feeling of moving through a story that slowly reveals its meaning over time.
